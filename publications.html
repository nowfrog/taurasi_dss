Unveiling Taurasi's Hidden Potential: A Wine Culture-Centered Framework for Sustainable Development and Circular Economy Transition in Historic Small Towns

Ciliberto, C., Calabrò, G., Caristi, G., Arbolino, R., & Ioppolo, G.

Sustainability, 17(13), 5704 • 2025

Abstract

This study presents a novel methodological framework for analyzing circular economy potential in historic small towns, using Taurasi as an illustrative case to demonstrate framework applicability rather than providing a comprehensive quantitative assessment. The primary contribution is methodological: developing an integrated analytical framework that combines urban metabolism analysis, stakeholder engagement theory, and heritage preservation strategies. The framework addresses the specific challenges of implementing circular economy principles in heritage-rich contexts where traditional data collection methods may be insufficient. This research provides a replicable methodology for future comprehensive empirical studies in similar contexts.

A Multi-Criteria Decision-Making Analysis of Waste Management Practices in Historical Small Towns: The Taurasi Case Study

Ioppolo, G., & Calabrò, G.

Atti della Accademia Peloritana dei Pericolanti - Classe di Scienze Fisiche, Matematiche e Naturali • Ongoing

Abstract

This research presents a comprehensive multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM) analysis of waste management strategies applicable to Historical Small Towns (HSTs), using the municipality of Taurasi as a pilot case study. The study addresses the unique challenges faced by these settlements in implementing effective waste management systems while respecting heritage preservation requirements and operating within constrained budgets.

The methodology integrates multiple evaluation criteria including economic feasibility, environmental impact, social acceptance, and compatibility with historical urban fabric. By applying MCDM techniques to the specific context of HSTs, the research identifies optimal intervention strategies that balance efficiency improvements with heritage conservation objectives.

Preliminary findings indicate that Taurasi's waste management system already demonstrates relatively advanced practices with a 67.77% differentiation rate. The analysis identifies opportunities for enhancing circular approaches through composting initiatives targeting organic fractions (259.62 tons annually) and localized glass recycling systems connected to the local wine industry's packaging requirements (91.66 tons annually). The study contributes to developing evidence-based decision support for policymakers in similar HST contexts.

Conference Paper

Sustainable Circularity: Tracing Taurasi's Historical Small Town Resilience

Ciliberto, C., Calabrò, G., Caristi, G., & Ioppolo, G.

XXXII Congresso Nazionale di Scienze Merceologiche
"Resilienza e sostenibilità nel cambiamento globale"
Lecce, Italy • September 19-20, 2024

Abstract

This paper examines the resilience mechanisms of Taurasi as a representative Italian Historical Small Town (HST) within the context of circular economy transition. The research investigates how traditional practices and community structures contribute to the settlement's adaptive capacity while identifying opportunities for enhancing circularity through targeted interventions.

The study applies resilience theory to understand how HSTs respond to disturbances, focusing on their capacity to absorb shocks, adapt to changing conditions, and potentially transform in response to persistent challenges. Rather than aiming simply to preserve historical form, this approach seeks to maintain essential functions and identity while enabling necessary adaptation to contemporary sustainability requirements.

Findings demonstrate that Taurasi's viticultural traditions and associated knowledge systems represent significant resilience assets that could inform contemporary circular approaches when appropriately integrated with modern techniques. The paper contributes to understanding how historical settlements can leverage their distinctive characteristics for sustainable development pathways.

Conference Paper

Analysing Italian Historical Small Towns: A Cultural and Geographic Mosaic of Identity

Ciliberto, C., Ioppolo, G., Caristi, G., & Calabrò, G.

7th International Conference on Finance, Economics, Management and IT Business (FEMIB 2025)
Porto, Portugal • April 5-6, 2025

Abstract

This paper develops a comprehensive typological framework for understanding Italian Historical Small Towns (HSTs), commonly known as "borghi," that integrates multiple classification dimensions to capture their complex nature. The research recognizes that these settlements represent multifaceted environments shaped by the interaction of physical geography, historical development patterns, and economic systems that collectively define their distinctive character.

The typological framework is structured around three fundamental analytical dimensions: the geographic dimension addressing physical positioning and topographic context; the historical-cultural dimension examining developmental chronology, architectural character, and urban morphological patterns; and the economic function dimension focusing on predominant productive activities and specialized production systems.

By applying this framework to sample settlements including Taurasi, the research demonstrates how different typological characteristics influence circular economy implementation potential. The geographic dimension shapes resource accessibility and environmental relationships, the historical-cultural dimension influences building adaptability and urban morphological constraints, while the economic dimension determines dominant material flows and valorization opportunities. This integrated understanding creates a foundation for developing circular economy strategies that are both theoretically sound and contextually appropriate.

Smart Transition in Historical Small Towns: First Insights from a Digital Material Flow Assessment in the Italian Case Study of Taurasi

Ciliberto, C., Calabrò, G., Caristi, G., & Ioppolo, G.

Research and Innovation Forum 2025
Competitiveness, growth and well-being through technology, innovation and education
April 24-25, 2025

Abstract

This paper presents first empirical insights from applying digital material flow assessment methodologies to the Historical Small Town (HST) of Taurasi. The research addresses the critical need for systematic quantification of resource flows within these distinctive settlement contexts as a foundation for evidence-based circular economy planning.

The methodology combines traditional Material Flow Analysis (MFA) approaches with digital data collection and analysis tools to create a comprehensive metabolic profile of the settlement. The assessment encompasses social, economic, environmental, and digital flows operating across micro and meso scales, revealing the complex interplay of resource dynamics characteristic of HSTs.

Preliminary findings demonstrate that Taurasi's resource flows reflect its distinctive character as an HST with strong viticultural traditions. The aging population creates specific resource needs and service requirements, while the dominance of small-scale wine production enterprises establishes particular patterns of resource utilization and waste generation. The digital assessment approach enables more precise identification of circular economy opportunities, including potential for urban-agricultural symbiosis between the urban center and surrounding agricultural production zones.

Digital Transformation for Circular Economy: Business Process Innovation in Historic Small Towns - The Taurasi Case Study

Ciliberto, C., Calabrò, G., Caristi, G., & Ioppolo, G.

Digital Transformation Society International Conference (DTS 2025)
Paris, France • May 22-23, 2025

Abstract

This paper explores how digital transformation can enable business process innovation for circular economy implementation in the distinctive context of Historic Small Towns (HSTs). Using Taurasi as a case study, the research examines the intersection of Industry 5.0 principles—emphasizing human-centrism, resilience, and sustainability—with the specific requirements and constraints of historical settlement contexts.

The study investigates how digital technologies can enhance rather than compromise historical character while enabling more efficient resource utilization and circular business models. Key areas of analysis include digital platforms for connecting local producers within circular supply chains, smart monitoring systems for waste stream optimization, and decision support tools that integrate heritage preservation requirements with sustainability objectives.

The research demonstrates that successful digital transformation in HST contexts requires approaches specifically tailored to local conditions, recognizing that technological solutions must respect cultural continuity while enabling innovation. Findings contribute to understanding how smaller historical settlements can leverage digital capabilities to accelerate circular transitions while maintaining their distinctive identity and heritage values.
