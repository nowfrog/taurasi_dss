APPLMATH 2025

International Workshop on Applied Mathematics

June 10-11, 2025 Messina, Italy

Description

The International Workshop on Applied Mathematics (APPLMATH 2025) brought together researchers and practitioners working on mathematical applications in various domains including sustainability, optimization, and decision support systems.

The H-SMA-CE team presented research on Multi-Criteria Decision-Making Analysis for waste management practices in historical small towns. The presentation focused on the development and application of MCDM methodologies to evaluate and optimize waste management strategies within the unique constraints of Historical Small Towns (HSTs).

The research demonstrated how mathematical optimization techniques can be adapted to address the specific challenges of heritage contexts, balancing efficiency improvements with preservation requirements. The presentation included empirical findings from the Taurasi case study, showcasing practical applications of the theoretical framework.

Presentation

Title: Multi-Criteria Decision-Making Analysis for Waste Management in Historical Small Towns

Presenter: Prof. Giuseppe Ioppolo

Research and Innovation Forum 2025

Competitiveness, growth and well-being through technology, innovation and education

April 24-25, 2025 International

Description

The Research and Innovation Forum 2025 addressed contemporary challenges at the intersection of competitiveness, growth, and well-being through technological innovation and education. The forum provided a platform for presenting cutting-edge research with practical implications for sustainable development.

The H-SMA-CE team presented first empirical insights from applying digital material flow assessment methodologies to the Historical Small Town of Taurasi. This research addressed the critical need for systematic quantification of resource flows within distinctive settlement contexts as a foundation for evidence-based circular economy planning.

The methodology combined traditional Material Flow Analysis (MFA) approaches with digital data collection and analysis tools to create a comprehensive metabolic profile of the settlement. The assessment encompassed social, economic, environmental, and digital flows operating across micro and meso scales, revealing how smart technologies can support sustainable transitions in heritage contexts.

XXXII Congresso Nazionale di Scienze Merceologiche

Resilienza e sostenibilitÃ  nel cambiamento globale

September 19-20, 2024 Lecce, Italy

Description

The XXXII National Congress of Commodity Sciences, held in Lecce under the theme "Resilience and Sustainability in Global Change," brought together Italian researchers working on sustainable development, circular economy, and resource management. The congress provided a national platform for presenting cutting-edge research on sustainability challenges and solutions.

The H-SMA-CE team presented research examining the resilience mechanisms of Taurasi as a representative Italian Historical Small Town (HST) within the context of circular economy transition. The study investigated how traditional practices and community structures contribute to the settlement's adaptive capacity while identifying opportunities for enhancing circularity through targeted interventions.

The presentation applied resilience theory to understand how HSTs respond to disturbances, focusing on their capacity to absorb shocks, adapt to changing conditions, and transform in response to persistent challenges. Rather than aiming simply to preserve historical form, this approach seeks to maintain essential functions and identity while enabling necessary adaptation. The paper was published in the conference proceedings (ISBN: 979-12-985593-1-8), pages 774-781.
